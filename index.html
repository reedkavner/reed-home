Hi, I'm Reed Kavner.

I'm an internet creator, comedy producer, and performer in New York City. I love collaborating with creative people on projects that take dumb ideas very seriously or find some fun in the ordinary.

My work has been featured in places like The New York Times, Gizmodo, and The Verge. In 2018, my TV pilot reed.computer was a TruTV Comedy Breakout finalist at the New York Television Festival.

I produce live shows across New York City, including my all-PowerPoint comedy show Next Slide Please.

Follow me on Twitter and Instagram, or get in touch at r@reed.me.

Recent Projects

Low Tide

A scripted comedy podcast I produce alongside a team of comedy writers, performers, and other creatives. A parody of NPR shows like All Things Considered, Low Tide is is the most popular radio show in the fictional seaside comunity of Conch County.

Instagram Filters

I've published a series of Instagram filters that use augmented reality to make your selfies and the world around you more fun. (I mean, your selfies are already *very* fun, but wouln't you like to turn your face into a veggie burger?)

Natasha Wears Clothes

A "comedic gallery experience" featuring original photos and charachter monologues from comedian Natasha Vaynblat.

Neil Cicierega "GIF Wall"

A chaotic interactive visualization for a preview party for Neil Cicierga's forthcoming album. Built in collaboration with Em Lazer-Walker and Andy Baio.

Backwards Hangman

Inspired by the Bill Wurtz video of the same name, Backwards Hangman was created for Mark Vigeant's Internet Explorers and debuted before a live audience (including Bill Wurtz himself).

Noodle Delight

A one-minute movie created by me and Mike Zakarian. An official selection of Quickie Fest 2019.

Zach Zimmerman's Clean Comedy

I worked with comedian Zach Zimmerman on the marketing and distrubution of his album, Clean Comedy. Clean Comedy debuted at #1 on the iTunes comedy chart and was a top-10 Billboard comdedy record.

@StephenColbot

Stephen Colbot is an AI trained on 1,100 monologues from The Late Show with Stephen Colbert. It uses Open AI's GPT-2 language model to generate a new monologue every few hours.

@WikiPlotBot

A Twitter bot that describes movies using only the links in their Wikipedia plot summaries.

Allergies or a Cold?

Compare your area's pollen count with its cold and flu index to diagnose your runny nose.

@fashionbot2000

A Twitter bot that uses machine learning to generate fashion insights and advice. A collaboration with comedian/illustrator/podcaster Max Wittert.

a++

Discover must-listen episodes from the best podcasts. A collaboration with Fresh Air editor Mooj Zadie and a small army of volunteer curators.

Greatest Hits

Billboard Magazine

As Director of Product at Billboard, I led the development of digital products for our readers and editors, including a major redesign of the Billboard Charts in 2018. I also acted as a digital producer for editorial and sponsored initiatives like the magazine's open letter to congress and the Webby-nominated Back to the Block series.

Rezhound

I built Rezhound to help me get tables at the country's toughest-to-book restaurants and to teach myself to program in Python. It has since gained a loyal following of foodie fans and has been featured everywhere from Gizmodo to The New York Frickin' Times.

Fav Forever

Longtime Twitter users were upset when the company replaced the beloved "favorite" star with a heart icon. Three hours after the change, I released Fav Forever, a Chrome extension to bring the stars back. Mashable and The Verge wrote about the extension later that day.
